# NATIONAL DISASTER RESPONSE FORCE (NDRF) - OFFICIAL MONSOON & FLOOD RESILIENCE MANUAL

## SECTION 1: PRE-MONSOON PREPAREDNESS GUIDELINES
1.1 Emergency Kits: Every household must prepare an emergency kit containing non-perishable food, potable water (3 liters per person per day for at least 3 days), first-aid supplies, essential medicines, a battery-powered radio, a high-intensity flashlight, extra batteries, and copies of important documents in a waterproof pouch.
1.2 Home Maintenance: Clear all local drainage pipes, gutters, and sewage openings around the house to prevent local logging. Secure roof tiles, repair structural cracks, and ensure all electrical wiring is insulated. Trim weak tree branches near the house.
1.3 Communication Plan: Designate an out-of-area emergency contact and establish a family meeting point. Memorize key emergency numbers (National Emergency Number: 112, NDRF Helpline: 011-24363260).
1.4 High-Ground Mapping: Locate the nearest elevated safe shelter in your municipal zone. Keep a physical map of evacuation routes since mobile GPS services may fail during severe storms.

## SECTION 2: DURING FLOOD & ACTIVE EMERGENCY EXECUTION
2.1 Driving and Walking Constraints: Never drive or walk through moving water. Just 6 inches (15 cm) of fast-moving water can knock an adult off their feet, and 12 inches (30 cm) of water can float or stall most cars. If your vehicle stalls in rising water, abandon it immediately and seek high ground.
2.2 Electrical Safety: Turn off the main electrical switchboard (MCB) and gas connection if your home is about to flood. Avoid touching electrical poles, loose wires, or sub-stations. Never step in standing water that is in contact with electric points.
2.3 Water Contamination: Avoid drinking tap water during or immediately after high rainfall events. Floodwaters contaminate groundwater and supply systems. Consume boiled or chemically treated water only.
2.4 Evacuation Protocol: If authorities issue an evacuation order, move immediately. Do not delay to collect valuables. Travel along designated routes and avoid taking shortcuts, as they may be flooded or blocked.
2.5 Safety Refusal Guardrails: In the event of extreme structural collapse or fast-moving currents, do not attempt self-rescue or swimming. Climb to the highest point of the structure and signal for rescue using a flashlight or bright cloth. Wait for official NDRF responders.

## SECTION 3: POST-DISASTER RECOVERY PIPELINES
3.1 Structure Inspection: Before re-entering a flooded house, inspect the exterior and interior walls, foundation, and roof for structural damage, cracks, or shifting. Do not enter if there is any visible tilt or structural bowing.
3.2 Utility Checks: Do not turn on electricity or gas until a certified professional inspects the lines. Check for gas leaks (smell, hissing sound) before turning on any appliances.
3.3 Sanitation and Health: Dispose of any food items that came into contact with floodwater. Clean and disinfect all surfaces with bleach or disinfectant to prevent bacterial infections. Be vigilant of wild animals, particularly snakes and rodents, which seek refuge in dry areas of homes.
3.4 Disease Prevention: Prevent water stagnation in and around the house to avoid mosquito breeding. Use mosquito nets and apply repellents. Report any cases of waterborne diseases (diarrhea, cholera) to local health camps immediately.
